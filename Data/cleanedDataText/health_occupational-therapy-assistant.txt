Program Overview:

    The Occupational Therapy Assistant program at USI helps individuals acquire the necessary skills to become independent in daily living activities. Students work under the guidance and collaboration of occupational therapists, learning the therapeutic use of self-care, work, and play/leisure activities to enhance development, prevent disability, and improve quality of life.

About the Program:

    - The program offers a 65-credit hour associate degree.
    - Consists of a 17-hour foundation in liberal arts and sciences (50% of USI University Core Curriculum) and 48 hours of occupational therapy assistant courses.
    - Students complete the 17-hour block of University Core Curriculum classes before enrolling in the program.
    - After acceptance, students complete the 42-credit hour didactic component and the 6-credit hour clinical component.
    - Includes 16 weeks of integrated practicum experiences alongside classroom and laboratory hours.
    - Full-time students can complete the associate of science degree in Occupational Therapy Assistant in five semesters and one summer.
    - Graduates are encouraged to pursue a Bachelor of Science degree in Health Services.

Accreditation:

    The Occupational Therapy Assistant program is accredited by the Accreditation Council for Occupational Therapy Education (ACOTE) of the American Occupational Therapy Association (AOTA).

Program Outcomes:

    - Upon successful completion of the program and National Board of Occupational Therapists (NBCOT) COTA examination, students are prepared for employment in the state of Indiana.
    - The state licensure requirements for employment outside of Indiana may vary.
