Endeavor Awards for Research & Creativity

    Abstracts

        An abstract is a brief summary of your project, typically fewer than 350 words.
        It is used for academic presentations to give a synopsis of the research project or a summary of a creativity project.
        You can find sample abstracts here.
        A typical abstract should include:
            - Initial statement addressing the problem, task, or goal of the project and how it will benefit you, your field, and the world.
        
    Creative Projects:
        - State why you chose to produce your particular work and how it expands the field you're working in.
        - Include information about the creative process and the final project.
        - Discuss why certain choices were made and what reactions you aimed to elicit from the audience.
        - Include an interpretation of your work and any reception it received.

    Scientific, Business, or Academic Area Projects:
        - State the specific problem addressed by your project.
        - Background statement to aid understanding, if necessary.
        - Detail the data, methodology, or techniques used in the project.
        - Provide your interpretation of the data.
