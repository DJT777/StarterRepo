Respiratory Therapy Program at University of Southern Indiana (USI)

Curriculum Outline:
- 120 credit hours leading to a Bachelor of Science degree in Respiratory Therapy.
- Includes 41 credit hours from selected shared core courses, 11 credit hours from electives, and 68 credit hours of required respiratory therapy courses.

Admission Requirements:
- Review of the admission application after completion of 29 hours of required courses.
- Must have completed 29 college credit hours specified in the respiratory therapy curriculum.
- General admission guidelines include completing all prerequisite courses with a grade of "C" or better.
- Applicants must apply and be accepted to USI and submit the Respiratory Therapy Program Application.
- Official transcripts from all undergraduate institutions attended must be submitted.
- Admission to the program is competitive based on GPA, healthcare experience, completion of prerequisite courses, and SAT/ACT scores if applicable.

Core Courses:
- Shared core courses include English, Math, Biology, Psychology, Communications, Chemistry, and others.

Respiratory Therapy Required Courses:
- Includes courses on Introduction to Respiratory, Respiratory Therapy Modalities, Cardiopulmonary Anatomy and Physiology, Pulmonary Diseases, Cardiopulmonary Pharmacology, Mechanical Ventilation, Emergency Respiratory Therapy Management, Pediatric and Newborn Respiratory Therapy, Pulmonary Diagnostics, Clinical Practice of Respiratory Therapy, Management and Leadership for Respiratory Therapy, Research in Respiratory Therapy, Critical Care Management, Pulmonary Rehabilitation, Professional Issues in Respiratory Therapy, and more.

Online Application is available for the program.
