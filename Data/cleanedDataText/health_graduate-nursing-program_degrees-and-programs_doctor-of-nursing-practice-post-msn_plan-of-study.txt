Doctor of Nursing Practice - Post MSN

    Plan of Study
    Contact: mwhand, Phone: 812-492-7807, Email provided on the site

Overview of the Program:

    Total of 78 graduate nursing credit hours
    Requires an MSN degree for eligibility
    Maximum of 42 credit hours from MSN degree can be applied towards the DNP degree
    Full-time students complete the required credit hours over five semesters, including one summer session
    Part-time students complete the required credit hours over eight semesters, including two summer sessions
    Hybrid course delivery approach with distance education methodologies and two on-campus Intensives
    On-campus Intensives held in August and April of each year, combining course instruction, faculty meetings, presentations, and socialization opportunities
    Sample Plans of Study available for students starting before 2020 and 2020 onwards

Curriculum Structure:

    Leadership and Organizational Systems Core Courses: Systems Leadership and Inter-Professional Collaboration, Health Care Policy and Strategic Planning, Resource Utilization in Health Care, Healthcare Technology and Informatics (15 credit hours)
    Evidence-Based Practice Core Courses: Theory and Practice, Analytical Methods for Population-Based Care, Cultural Diversity (9 credit hours)
    Specialty Practice Courses: Critical Appraisal of Practice I, Synthesis of Nursing Practice, Capstone Project I-VI (12 credit hours)
    Total Program Hours: 36 credit hours
