Food and Nutrition Scholarships and Aid

Scholarships Available:

1. Deaconess Health System Health Professions Scholarship
    - Number of Awards: 6
    - Scholarship Amount: $1000
    - Selection Process: Faculty selection
    - Application Criteria: Full-time students in good academic standing (3.0 GPA or higher) at USI who have been accepted into the diagnostic medical sonography, food and nutrition, or radiologic and imaging sciences program. Recipients must display professional attributes consistent with the mission of Deaconess Health System and demonstrate financial need.

2. Food and Nutrition Academic Achievement Award
    - Number of Awards: 1
    - Scholarship Amount: $75.00
    - Selection Process: Faculty selection
    - Application Criteria: Recipient must be a Food and Nutrition major and a member of the Food and Nutrition Student Organization. Must participate in a minimum of three FNSO meetings/activities during the membership year and maintain a GPA of 3.0 or higher. Not renewable.

3. Food and Nutrition Student Organization Scholarship
    - Number of Awards: 1
    - Scholarship Amount: $50.00
    - Selection Process: Faculty selection
    - Application Criteria: Recipient must be a Food and Nutrition major and a member of the Food and Nutrition Student Organization. Must participate in a minimum of three FNSO meetings/activities during the membership year and maintain a GPA of 3.0 or higher. Not renewable.

4. Trilogy Emerging Student Leaders in Long-term Care Scholarship
    - Number of Awards: 4
    - Scholarship Amount: $550.00
    - Selection Process: Faculty selection
    - Application Criteria: Full-time students at USI enrolled in an undergraduate pre-clinical program related to gerontology, geriatrics, long-term care, and/or long-term care administration. Preference given to students with work experience or volunteer service in gerontology and those with junior, senior, or graduate student status. Must have a GPA of 3.0 or higher. One-page essay describing career goals required. Renewable based on eligibility.

For more information on these scholarships and how to apply, contact the Food and Nutrition program.
