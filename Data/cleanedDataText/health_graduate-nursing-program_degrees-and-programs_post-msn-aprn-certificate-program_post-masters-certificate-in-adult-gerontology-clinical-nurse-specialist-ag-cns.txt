Post Master's Certificate in Adult-Gerontology Clinical Nurse Specialist (AG-CNS)

About the Program:
The Adult-Gerontology Clinical Nurse Specialist program is designed for advanced clinicians with a master's degree. The program prepares nurses to function as expert clinicians, leaders, researchers, educators, and consultants in three spheres: patient/family, nursing personnel, and organization system. AG-CNS graduates are trained to provide specialized healthcare services to individuals across the lifespan, focusing on health promotion, disease prevention, and management of acute and chronic illnesses. They can work in various clinical settings, including hospitals, community agencies, long-term care facilities, home care, and physician offices. The program requires a total of 545 clinical hours.

Program Objectives:
- Provide expert nursing therapeutics for complex patients, promoting health, well-being, and quality of life.
- Manage integration and coordination of patient care services across the healthcare continuum.
- Design and implement educational programs for patients and staff to promote positive healthcare outcomes.
- Consult and collaborate with the healthcare team to support the delivery of quality patient care.
- Utilize research methodology and evidence to investigate, promote, and evaluate nursing therapies for positive patient care outcomes.
- Model responsibility for professional development, integrity, accountability, competence, and credentialing as a clinical nurse specialist.

Plan of Study Sample:
- Clinical Core Courses:
  - NURS 617: Advanced Concepts of Pathophysiology (3 credit hours)
  - NURS 618: Advanced Health Assessment (3 credit hours)
  - NURS 622: Clinical Pharmacology for Advanced Nursing Practice (3 credit hours)

- Adult-Gerontology Clinical Nurse Practitioner Specialty Courses:
  - NURS 645: Advanced Nursing Assessment and Intervention I (5 credit hours)
  - NURS 646: Advanced Nursing Assessment and Intervention II (5 credit hours)
  - NURS 647: Advanced Nursing Assessment and Intervention III (5 credit hours)

Tuition Information and Other Details:
- Gainful employment information can be found on the gainful employment website, as required by the Higher Education Opportunity Act.
- Out-of-state students interested in pursuing a distance learning degree at USI should check their availability, as the online programs have limited recognition in certain states.
- English proficiency requirements apply to non-native English speakers.
- The nursing programs at the University of Southern Indiana are accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education.

Admission Information:
- The program admits students once a year.
- Fall and spring courses are 16 weeks long, while summer courses are 10 weeks long.
- No campus visits are required, but student visits can be arranged through the USI Office of Admission.

For more information, visit the USI website or contact the College of Nursing and Health Professions.
