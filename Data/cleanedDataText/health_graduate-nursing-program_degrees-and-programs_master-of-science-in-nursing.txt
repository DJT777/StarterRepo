Master of Science in Nursing Online Program at the University of Southern Indiana

Program Overview:

- 100% online program
- Designed for practicing nurses to advance their careers
- Offers specialty tracks in various areas of nursing
- Ranked among the best online graduate nursing programs by U.S. News and World Report

Specialty Tracks:

- Adult-Gerontology Acute Care Nurse Practitioner
- Adult-Gerontology Clinical Nurse Specialist
- Family Nurse Practitioner
- Healthcare Systems Leadership
- Nursing Education Leadership
- Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner

Admission Requirements:

- Baccalaureate degree in nursing (BSN) from a nationally-accredited nursing program and a regionally-accredited college or university
- Minimum cumulative baccalaureate GPA of 3.00 on a 4.00 scale
- Completion of a course in undergraduate statistics with a grade of C or better
- Unencumbered US RN license in all states of practice
- Recommended: one year full-time or two thousand hours of clinical practice as an RN within the last five years

Application Procedures:

- Submit an online application and $40 application fee
- Submit transcripts to Graduate Studies (official transcripts only from the institution where the highest degree was earned, unofficial transcripts from other institutions attended can be submitted via email)
- International applicants may be required to submit additional information

Start Dates and Deadlines:

- Spring Semester (January): Application Deadline - September 1st, Document Deadline - September 15th
- Summer Semester (May): Application Deadline - February 1st, Document Deadline - February 15th
- Fall Semester (August): Application Deadline - August 1st, Document Deadline - August 15th

Accreditation: The master's degree program in nursing at the University of Southern Indiana is accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education.
