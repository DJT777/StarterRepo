Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering at University of Southern Indiana (USI)

Overview:

- Program: Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering (BSME)
- Accreditation: Accredited by the Engineering Accreditation Commission (EAC) of ABET (http://www.abet.org)
- Description: The BSME program at USI prepares students to conceive and design mechanical devices and systems for various applications, such as transportation, HVAC, manufacturing, and consumer products.
- Importance of Math: Strong math skills, including proficiency in calculus, are crucial for success in this program.
- Course Requirements: Students can refer to the course requirements outlined for a baccalaureate degree in mechanical engineering.
- Alternative Programs: For students interested in engineering-related programs that are less math-intensive, USI offers advanced manufacturing or industrial supervision programs, which focus on hands-on skills.

Opportunities:

- Career Outlook: Mechanical engineers have diverse job opportunities with a median annual pay of $87,370 (U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, 2018).
- Practical Experience: Students are encouraged to gain practical experience through extracurricular engineering activities, internships, and co-ops.
- Notable Achievements: USI's engineering team designed and built a CubeSat for NASA, which was launched into orbit in 2019. USI College of Engineering also won funding to design and monitor a satellite for NASA.
- Faculty Expertise: Students benefit from instruction by experienced faculty, many of whom have successful careers in industry. Class sizes are typically limited to facilitate interaction between students and instructors.
- Student Support: USI maintains a student-faculty ratio of 16:1 and emphasizes an open-door policy, ensuring that students have easy access to their professors.
