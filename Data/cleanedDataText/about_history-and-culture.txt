History and Culture - University of Southern Indiana (USI)

Establishment and Growth:

- The University of Southern Indiana was established in 1965 to meet the need for public higher education in southwestern Indiana.
- It began as a regional campus of Indiana State University and opened on September 15, 1965.
- In 1967, Southern Indiana Higher Education, Inc. (SIHE) raised funds to acquire 1,400 acres for the Mid-America University Center.
- Groundbreaking took place on June 22, 1968.
- Since September 1969, the University has occupied 330 acres, with the majority donated by SIHE.
- On April 16, 1985, legislation was signed to make USI a separate state university, effective from July 1, 1985.
- On June 30, 2008, SIHE transferred ownership of over 900 acres of land and remaining assets to the USI Foundation.

Former Presidents:

- Dr. David L. Rice was the first president, serving from 1967 to 1994.
- Dr. H. Ray Hoops served as president from 1994 to 2009.
- Dr. Linda L. M. Bennett served as president from 2009 to 2018.

USI Alma Mater:

- The USI alma mater, titled "Constant, Faithful and True," was premiered by the award-winning USI Chamber Choir during the Faculty Convocation on October 12, 2018.
- The music was composed by Daniel R. Craig, with lyrics by Dr. David G. O'Neil.
- The alma mater represents the university's dedication and loyalty.
- It symbolizes the meeting of the North and South, East and West, in Evansville, and the loyalty of students and alumni to USI.
