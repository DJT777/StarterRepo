College of Nursing and Health Professions - Dental Hygiene

Degree and Program Information:
- USI's dental hygiene program was the first bachelor's degree dental hygiene program in Indiana.
- Offers a curriculum that includes informative courses, laboratory and clinical experiences, and community oral health education experiences.
- Prepares graduates to deliver oral healthcare services and education in various practice settings.
- Dental hygiene students gain hands-on experience at USI's Dental Hygiene Clinic, a state-of-the-art facility located on campus.

Program Accreditation:
- Accredited by the Commission on Dental Accreditation of the American Dental Association.
- Commission on Dental Accreditation is a specialized accrediting body recognized by the US Department of Education.

Licensure and Certification Requirements:
- Individuals must pass the National Board Dental Hygiene examination, a clinical exam, and a state jurisprudence exam to be licensed in Indiana.
- Licensure requirements in other states have not been determined by the university.

Career Opportunities:
- Graduates have various career options in private dental practices, public health clinics, schools, hospitals, research, and educational institutions.
- Dental hygienist employment projected to grow 9% from 2021 to 2031, faster than the average for all occupations.
- USI dental hygiene program has an outstanding job placement rate and success on licensure examinations.

Unique Program Features:
- Small class sizes with a low student-to-faculty ratio.
- Experienced faculty with clinical practice experience.
- Outstanding community service opportunities.
- Diverse client population at the Dental Hygiene Clinic.
