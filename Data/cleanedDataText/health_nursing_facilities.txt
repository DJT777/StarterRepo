College of Nursing and Health Professions

Nursing Programs:

- Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN)
- Registered Nurse to Bachelor of Science in Nursing (RN to BSN)
- Graduate Nursing Programs (Master of Science in Nursing, Doctor of Nursing, Post MSN certificates)

Benefits of USI's Nursing Programs:

- High-quality education at an affordable price
- Smaller class sizes and a supportive learning environment
- Expert faculty with advanced nursing degrees and certifications
- Preparation for advanced nursing roles, leadership positions, public health, academia, and more
- Options for online learning and specialized areas of study

Nursing Program Highlights:

- 100% NCLEX pass rate (May 2023), above the national rate of 87.6%
- Median annual salary of $77,600 (BLS 2023)
- Projected job growth of 6% through 2031
- Employment within 6 months of graduation for 100% of students

Programs Offered:

- Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN)
- RN to BSN (Registered Nurse to Bachelor of Science in Nursing)
- Master of Science in Nursing (Multiple specializations)
- Doctor of Nursing Practice
- Post MSN certificates in various specializations

Accreditation: 
The Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN), Master's degree program in nursing, Doctor of Nursing Practice (DNP) program, and Post MSN APRN Certificate program at the University of Southern Indiana are accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education.
