College of Nursing and Health Professions - Dental Hygiene Program

Admission Requirements:
- Limited to 24 students per academic year.
- Admission ranking based on the student's grade point average in prerequisite courses.
- Eligibility for dental hygiene licensure and ability to fulfill clinical practice requirements.

Prerequisite Courses:
- Completion of prerequisite courses with a minimum cumulative grade point average of 2.8 (4.0 scale).
- All prerequisite courses must be completed prior to beginning the Dental Hygiene professional curriculum.
- All prerequisite courses must be completed with a grade of C or higher.

Required Courses:
- Two Tracks are available for completion of required courses: Track I - Entry-Level and Track II - Degree Completion.
- Both tracks include courses in mathematics, communication, anatomy and physiology, composition, medical terminology, sociology, and more.
- Track III - Accelerated Entry-Level is available for students who have already completed an accredited Associate Degree Dental Hygiene Program.

Application Process:
- Acceptance to the University of Southern Indiana is required before applying to the Dental Hygiene Program.
- The Dental Hygiene application will be considered after acceptance to the University.
- Submit an application by February 1st of the desired admission year.

Important Announcement:
- Starting Fall 2023, the course BIOL 272 Medical Microbiology must be completed as a 4-hour course. This affects the upcoming application cycle for the 2024-2025 academic year.

How to Apply:
- Ensure you meet the prerequisites and admission requirements stated above.
- Submit an application to the Dental Hygiene Program by February 1st of the desired admission year.
