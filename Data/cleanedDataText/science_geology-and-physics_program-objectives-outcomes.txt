Geology, Physics, and Environmental Science Department

- Program Objectives:
    - Find and evaluate data, models, hypotheses, and conclusions published in scientific literature.
    - Apply knowledge and techniques from various scientific fields to solve problems.
    - Conduct independent scientific investigations.
    - Present scientific data, observations, interpretations, and conclusions effectively.
    - Be aware of scientific issues that affect society and recognize ethical issues.
    - Collaborate with others to solve scientific problems and engage in lifelong learning.

Mission of Geology & Physics:
- Advancing education and knowledge, enhancing civic and cultural awareness, and fostering partnerships through comprehensive outreach programs.
- Hands-on experience in laboratory and fieldwork, technical writing and presentation skills, and professional growth opportunities.
- Prepare graduates for success in postgraduate academic programs or meet the demands of the workforce.

Program Objectives and Outcomes:
- Defined by faculty members based on rigorous assessment and review of programs.
- Five overarching program objectives:
    1. Find and evaluate scientific literature.
    2. Apply knowledge and techniques to solve scientific problems.
    3. Conduct independent scientific investigations.
    4. Make cogent presentations of scientific data.
    5. Collaborate with others and engage in lifelong learning.

Program Vision & Learning Outcomes:
- Different for each degree program offered in Geology & Physics (Biophysics, Environmental Science, Geology, Physics).
- Emphasis on unique knowledge, skills, and learning outcomes for each program.
- Detailed vision and learning outcomes for each degree program provided on the site.
