Occupational Therapy Program

    Degree and Program:

        College of Nursing and Health Professions offers the Occupational Therapy program.

    Program Admission:

        The program is for students with a bachelor's degree ready to apply for the entry-level graduate program in Occupational Therapy.
        No preference for the major of the bachelor's degree as long as the required prerequisites courses are completed.
        The best undergraduate major should be selected based on the student's interests and the area they want to work in after obtaining the degree.

    Tuition and Fees:

        For information about tuition, fees, and other costs, visit the financial aid section on the USI website.

    Location of Program:

        Stone Family Center for Health Sciences in Evansville, Indiana.

    Length of Program:

        Two years, including summers.

    Structure of Program:

        The program is designed as a full-time experience, including academic and fieldwork portions.

    Curriculum:

        The program offers a Master of Science in Occupational Therapy (MSOT).
        The curriculum consists of 75 credit hours.
        For detailed course information, refer to the Bulletin Degree Planner available on the USI website.
