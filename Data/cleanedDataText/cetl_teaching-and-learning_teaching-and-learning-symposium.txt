Teaching and Learning Symposium

- 7th Annual Celebration of Teaching and Learning Symposium
- Date and Time: Thursday, February 16, 2023, from 9:15 am to 2:45 pm CST
- Location: University Center East, Evansville, IN

Program:

- Presentation Abstracts: Available in PDF format
- Abstracts: Available in SOAR (USI's Scholarly Open Access Repository)
- Keynote Slides: Accessible online
- Handout: Provided during the event

Description:

- The Symposium is sponsored by the University of Southern Indiana's Center for Excellence in Teaching and Learning (CETL).
- It serves as a platform for showcasing works focusing on improving and transforming student learning, academic success, and curriculum in higher education.
- The Symposium encourages sharing teaching and learning efforts as the Scholarship of Teaching and Learning (SoTL) or Teaching Practice.
- Dr. Sushilla Knottenbelt from the University of New Mexico will deliver the keynote presentation during the luncheon.
- The program features lightning presentations, standard presentations, and poster presentations.

Benefits of Participation:

- Engage with participants from various disciplines and areas of work.
- Share and receive feedback on teaching and learning efforts.
- Spark new ideas and make teaching scholarship visible.
- Establish connections and strengthen existing ones.
- Gain knowledge of evidence-based strategies and technologies.
- Foster reflection on teaching practice and student learning.

Keynote Speaker:

- Dr. Sushilla Knottenbelt from the University of New Mexico will deliver a keynote presentation titled "Lessons from the Student Experience Project: Low lift strategies to increase student engagement, improve educational outcomes and reduce equity gaps."

Event Schedule:

- 9:15 am: Registration and Coffee
- 9:40 am: Welcome
- 10:00-10:45 am: Standard Presentations
- 11:00-11:50 am: Standard and Lightning Presentations
- 12:00-1:15 pm: Keynote Luncheon
- 1:15-1:50 pm: Poster Presentations and Dessert
- 2:00-2:45 pm: Standard Presentations
