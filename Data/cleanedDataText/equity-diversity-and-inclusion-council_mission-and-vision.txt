Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion at the University of Southern Indiana (USI)

Mission and Vision:

- Mission: The Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion Council (EDIC) of USI aims to identify, understand, act upon, and evaluate initiatives to promote inclusion, representation, involvement, and empowerment of diverse communities across campus.
- Vision: EDIC envisions creating an equitable environment that encourages challenging thought and practices while embracing individual uniqueness to ensure the representation, involvement, and empowerment of all communities on campus.

What is meant by Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion:

- A diverse, equitable, and inclusive campus environment is a collective responsibility.
- USI is committed to equity and serves as a champion of diversity and inclusion, striving for a positive and enriching campus experience for all.
- The definition of diversity includes various characteristics such as race, color, nationality, ideology, ethnicity, class, religion, disability, veteran status, age, sex, gender identity, gender expression, and sexual orientation.
- Inclusion and equity require integrating multiple cultural values and perspectives into teaching, decision-making, and all levels of the university.
- Creating a welcoming and supportive atmosphere for all community members and ensuring organizational structures support and reflect these ideals is crucial.
- The focus on equity, diversity, and inclusion is a collective effort that involves everyone.
