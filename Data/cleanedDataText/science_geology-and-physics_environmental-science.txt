Environmental Science at the Pott College of Science, Engineering, and Education

Degree Program:
- Environmental Science
    - Interdisciplinary approach to studying human interactions with the natural and built environment
    - Rigorous introduction to the hydrologic cycle and its relevance to human-environment interactions
    - Study of connections between the geosphere, atmosphere, hydrosphere, and biosphere
    - Examination of natural resource use and exploitation, as well as the environmental impact of population growth and energy needs
    - Integration of scientific principles across disciplines
    - Exploration of ecological interactions and threats to water resources
    - Study of economics, ethics, public policy, sociology, and sustainability
    - Emphasis on analysis and decision-making skills for environmental assessment and management
    - Exposure to state-of-the-art measurement and analytical techniques for contaminants in water and biological materials

Goals:
- Consistent with University's mission of engaged learning and advancing education and knowledge
- Broad understanding of environmental science
- Involvement in projects addressing cultural awareness and environmental problem-solving

Career Opportunities:
- Graduate and professional schools
- Natural resources careers
- Consulting firms and industries specializing in water resources
- Government agencies responsible for energy regulation and land use planning
- Non-profit organizations

Degree Requirements:
- Housed within the Department of Geology and Physics
- Completion of 56 credit hours of environmental science coursework
- Major coursework, supporting natural and social science classes, and University Core Curriculum (Core 39) requirements

What can you do with an Environmental Science Degree from USI?
- Environmental Consulting: Water and soil testing, pollution assessment and clean-up, resource exploration, repurposing of urban lands, planning and development programs
- Government/Agency: Research and development in a variety of fields
- Natural Resource Management: Minimize environmental impacts of resource recovery, conduct responsible reclamation activities
- Graduate Studies: Pursue advanced degrees in environmental health, resource management, land-use planning, engineering, law, and public policy.
