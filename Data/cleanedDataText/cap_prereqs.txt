    Course Prerequisites & Program Eligibility:
        High school students entering their junior or senior year are eligible for CAP enrollment.
        Sophomores in CAP-aligned career and technical education courses or the third year of a world language are also eligible to apply for CAP.
        Enrollment information will come from the student's school via the counseling office or directly from the CAP instructor.

    Criteria for most classes:
        GPA must be 2.5 or higher on a 4.0 scale (weighted or unweighted, based upon the school).
        Students previously in CAP must have received a grade of C or better to enroll in further coursework in the same subject area.
        Students must be enrolled in a CAP course offered at one of the participating high schools.

    Additional requirements for specific courses:
        Math 111, 112, 114: SAT/PSAT Math score of 550 or higher (Math 111/112) or 530 or higher (Math 114), ACT/PreACT Math score of 22 or higher (Math 111/112) or 21 or higher (Math 114), or placement through the Accuplacer exam.
        English 101: Cumulative high school GPA ≥ 3.0 and no PSAT/SAT/ACT scores, or cumulative high school GPA ≥ 3.0 and ACT English score of 17 or higher or PSAT/SAT EBRW score of 460 or higher, or placement through the timed placement essay.
        Chemistry 142, 143: Consent of instructor.
        Psychology 201: University Reading Level (URL) required, attained through specified SAT/PSAT/ACT scores or placement test.
        Radio/Television 150: Completion of required studio hours (50).
        Social Work 224: English 101 or concurrent enrollment.
        World Languages (French, German, Spanish): Consent of instructor (203), enrollment in 204 requires completion of 203 with a grade of C or higher and consent of instructor.

    Students with Disabilities:
        Students with disabilities who meet the eligibility standards described above are encouraged to enroll in CAP.
        High schools retain responsibility for providing and coordinating any accommodations or services based on the student's individual education plan (IEP), 504 Plan, or similar educational plan.
        Students in fully online or hybrid courses should be aware that some accommodations approved for traditional USI classroom courses may not apply.
