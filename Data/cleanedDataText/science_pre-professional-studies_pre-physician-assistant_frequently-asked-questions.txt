Pre-Physician Assistant Frequently Asked Questions

    Pott College of Science, Engineering, and Education

Preferred Majors:
While there is no specific major required for admission to PA school, Biology and Biochemistry offer the most direct preparation for your application.

GPA Requirement:
Most programs require a minimum GPA of 3.0 for application, but the average accepted GPA is usually higher. Aim for a 3.5 GPA to be competitive at all the schools you're interested in.

Required Pre-Requisite Courses:
Pre-requisite courses vary between PA schools. Some common required courses include Human Anatomy & Physiology (I and II), Microbiology & Lab, General Chemistry (I and II), Organic Chemistry (I and II), Statistics, Introduction to Psychology, Lifespan Developmental Psychology or Abnormal Psychology, and Medical Terminology.

Start Date of PA School:
PA schools have different start terms and lengths. Most programs last around 2 years, but the start dates can vary. Some programs start in January, May, or August. Research different schools to understand their timelines and requirements.

Application Process:
Most PA schools use the CASPA centralized system for applications, transcript submission, and letters of recommendation. Application cycles and requirements vary between schools, so be aware of the deadlines for each program you plan to apply to.

MCAT or GRE:
Most PA programs require the Graduate Record Exam (GRE), but some may accept MCAT scores in place of the GRE. Contact the Admissions office of the schools you're interested in for specific details.

Shadowing and Patient Contact:
Shadowing and patient contact experiences are important to expose yourself to the field. Requirements vary among PA schools, with some requiring a few shadowing experiences and others requiring a significant number of documented hours of direct patient care. Research the requirements of schools you're interested in to plan accordingly.

Tips for Applying:
- Ensure all information on your application is accurate.
- Request transcripts from all academic institutions in a timely manner.
- Pay attention to the personal statement as an opportunity to stand out.
- Begin the application process early to meet important deadlines.
- Choose letter writers carefully and provide them with enough time to write letters.
- Communicate your application deadlines clearly to your letter writers.
- Get involved in extracurricular activities to showcase your well-roundedness.

Contact:
For more information or specific inquiries, contact the Pott College of Science, Engineering, and Education.

Note: The information provided is a summary of the frequently asked questions. Please refer to the official university website or contact the relevant department for the most up-to-date and detailed information.
