Romain College of Business - Finance Program

Program Objectives and Student Outcomes

    Program Objectives:
        Provide high-quality finance education for graduates to contribute to the finance community.
        Establish a foundation for lifelong learning.

    Student Outcomes:
        Discipline-Specific Knowledge:
            Understand the role of financial management in business firms and the essentials of corporate finance.
            Apply the concept of the time value of money, capital budgeting techniques, and theory of capital structure.
            Understand the operation of security markets and the roles of businesses and investors.
            Apply valuation models to estimate the price of financial assets and measure risk.
            Understand financial markets, institutions, and the risks involved in global business.

        Skills:
            Analytical Problem-Solving Skill: Use accounting and financial data to solve complex business problems.
            Critical Thinking Skill: Formulate rational assumptions, identify implications, and draw conclusions on finance-related issues.
