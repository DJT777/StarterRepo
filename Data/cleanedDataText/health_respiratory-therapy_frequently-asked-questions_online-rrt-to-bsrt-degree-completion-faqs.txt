Online Learning at the University of Southern Indiana (USI)

RRT to BS in Respiratory Therapy Program

    Program Overview
        Complete in as few as 4 semesters
        100% online program
        $345 per credit hour
        Currently accepting applications
        Apply now for the deadline on 10/06/23 and start class on 10/23/23
        Upgrade your RRT to a BSRT degree

    Program Highlights
        Accredited program by the Commission on Accreditation for Respiratory Care (CoARC)
        Convenient and affordable online program for working professionals
        Individualized plan of study based on prior learning and professional experience
        Flexible class schedules with six start dates per year
        Accelerated or regular format options
        All courses are 7 weeks in duration
        Care for patients with advanced skills and step into leadership in respiratory therapist jobs
        Prepare for advanced clinical respiratory therapist jobs
        Gain skills in critical thinking, problem-solving, time management, communication, and practice
        Increase earning potential and opportunities for advancement

    Admission Requirements
        Completion of an associate degree in a CoARC accredited respiratory therapy program
        Cumulative grade point average of 2.3 on a 4.0 scale
        RRT credential through the National Board for Respiratory Care (NBRC)
        Submission of official transcripts
        Non-native English speakers must provide an official TOEFL or IELTS score

    Program and Graduation Requirements
        Transfer of credits earned at an accredited community college or university is allowed
        No limitation on the number of credits that may transfer into USI
        A minimum of 30 credit hours must be earned from USI
        Core respiratory therapy courses and non-respiratory support courses
        Total program requirements: 120 credits
        Grades of "C" or better required in each REST course

    Credit for Prior Learning
        Students may receive credit for professional certification in a respiratory therapy specialty
        Validation with a nationally recognized certification

    Program Goals and Learning Outcomes
        Program goals include providing advanced knowledge, skills, and attributes in leadership, management, education, research, or advanced clinical practice in respiratory therapy
        Learning outcomes include demonstrating professional behaviors, critical thinking skills, evaluating evidence-based practices, and more

    Application Deadline and Start Dates
        Six application deadlines per year
        Spring, summer, and fall semesters
        Multiple start dates available

    Tuition and Fees
        $345 per credit hour

    Financial Assistance
        Student financial assistance available
