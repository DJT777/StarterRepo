USI College of Nursing and Health Professions

Diagnostic Medical Sonography Program

Program Overview:
- The sonography program at USI consists of didactic courses, laboratory work, and clinical experience.
- The curriculum includes 1.5 years of pre-requisite general education courses and 2.5 years of professional education.
- After completing the program, students receive a Bachelor of Science degree in Diagnostic Medical Sonography.
- Graduates are eligible to apply for credentialing examinations administered by the American Registry for Diagnostic Medical Sonography (ARDMS).

Accreditation:
- USI's Diagnostic Medical Sonography program is accredited by the Commission on Accreditation of Allied Health Education Programs.
- Accreditation is specific to the Abdominal-Extended, Obstetrics and Gynecology, Vascular, and Cardiac concentrations.

Admissions Information:
- The program accepts a limited number of students each year, with the exact number determined by USI and its clinical affiliates. Currently, 12 students are accepted for each beginning class.
- Admission to the program is highly competitive, with an average of over 200 students majoring in Radiologic and Imaging Sciences and approximately 25 students applying and interviewing each year.

Employment Outlook:
- The employment of diagnostic medical sonographers is projected to grow by 12% from 2019 to 2029.
- There is increasing demand for diagnostic imaging and therapeutic technology, creating job opportunities.
- Sonography is becoming an attractive alternative to radiologic procedures, as it does not involve radiation and has fewer side effects.
- Sonography technology is expected to evolve rapidly, leading to new sonography procedures and opportunities in the field.
- Hospitals, offices of physicians, and medical and diagnostic laboratories are the main employers of sonographers.

Earnings Potential:
- The median annual wage for diagnostic medical sonographers was $74,320 in 2019.
