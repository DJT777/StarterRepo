The Pott College of Science, Engineering, and Education at the University of Southern Indiana offers programs in various fields. These include:

- Biology
- Chemistry and Biochemistry
- Engineering
- Geology, Physics, and Environmental Science
- Kinesiology and Sport
- Mathematical Sciences
- Teacher Education

The Pre-Professional Studies programs within the college prepare students for medical and healthcare-related careers. These programs include:

- Pre-Medicine
- Pre-Dentistry
- Pre-Chiropractic
- Pre-Optometry
- Pre-Physician Assistant
- Pre-Pharmacy
- Pre-Occupational Therapy
- Pre-Physical Therapy
- Pre-Veterinary
- Medical Laboratory Science

Students interested in pursuing a career in veterinary medicine can find the necessary information and resources at the college. The Doctor of Veterinary Medicine degree typically requires at least two years of undergraduate work (usually four) and four years in veterinary school. The specific undergraduate requirements vary from school to school, so it is important for students to familiarize themselves with the requirements of the veterinary schools they wish to attend.

The college provides a Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) page for Pre-Veterinary students, where they can find answers to common questions about the path to veterinary school.

Students interested in Pre-Veterinary preparation can contact the following faculty members for more information:

- Biology: Dr. T. Kyle R. Mara, Associate Professor of Biology
- Chemistry & Biochemistry: Dr. Kenneth E. Walsh, Associate Professor of Chemistry and Chair of the Chemistry and Biochemistry Department.
