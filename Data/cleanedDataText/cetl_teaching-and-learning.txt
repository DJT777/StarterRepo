Making Teaching & Learning Visible:
The Center for Excellence in Teaching and Learning (CETL) offers programs and services to support faculty in their teaching and learning endeavors. These include:

- Celebration of Teaching & Learning Symposia
- Scholarship of Teaching and Learning (SoTL) Fellows Program
- FACT Program (Formative Assessment of Classroom Teaching): Provides mid-semester student feedback.
- COPUS Classroom Observations: Uses observation protocol to collect non-evaluative information on teaching practices and student engagement.
- Teaching Nests: Opportunity to observe and reflect on teaching practices by visiting other instructors' classes.
- Faculty Learning Communities (FLCs) in teaching and learning.
- Consultations and classroom observations: Offers guidance on course development, specific teaching and learning topics, syllabus reviews, and the scholarship of teaching and learning (SoTL).
- Thank A Faculty or Staff Member program: Allows students to express gratitude to individuals who made a difference in their educational experience.

CETL partners with various units across USI, such as Online Learning, Information Technology, Rice Library, University Division, Institutional Analytics Office, and Office of Sponsored Programs and Research, to provide support in these areas.

Support for Teaching During COVID-19:
The Provost's Office provides guidance for faculty in response to the COVID-19 pandemic, including an updated syllabus template. CETL also offers webinars and resources to support teaching during this time.

CETL's Mission:
CETL supports USI's education mission by developing and implementing programs for faculty and instructors to enhance teaching and improve student learning and success. Areas of focus include best practices in instructional methods, innovations in teaching and learning, scholarship of teaching and learning, student-centered and inclusive learning environments, effective use of instructional technologies, and assessment and evaluation of teaching and student learning.
