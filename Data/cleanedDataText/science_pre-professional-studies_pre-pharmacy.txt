Pre-Pharmacy Program at the Pott College of Science, Engineering, and Education

At the University of Southern Indiana (USI), the Pre-Pharmacy program is offered through the Pott College of Science, Engineering, and Education. The program provides excellent preparation for students aspiring to pursue a career in Pharmacy, which offers various opportunities in fields such as retail, research, and hospital settings.

Admission Requirements:
- Strong foundation in science and math
- Bachelor's degree in biology, chemistry, or biochemistry (recommended)

Pharmacy School Pathways:
USI has established pathway partnerships with Manchester University and Sullivan University for expedited opportunities in Pharmacy studies.
- Sullivan University: 3+3 Pathway, allowing students to complete a Bachelor's degree at USI and a PharmD at Sullivan University School of Pharmacy in as few as 6 years.
- Manchester University: Offered pathways include Doctor of Pharmacy (PharmD), Dual Degree Program (Doctor of Pharmacy and Master of Science in Pharmacogenomics), and a 1-Year, On-Campus Master of Science in Pharmacogenomics.

FAQs and Resources:
USI offers a FAQ page to address common questions regarding the path to Pharmacy School, applications, and the PCAT (Pharmacy College Admission Test).

Success Stories:

Dr. Joe Kawa '12: Clinical Specialist Pharmacist, Omnicare
