Bachelor of Science in Manufacturing Engineering

Pott College of Science, Engineering, and Education offers the following programs:

- Biology
- Chemistry and Biochemistry
- Engineering
- Geology, Physics, and Environmental Science
- Kinesiology and Sport
- Mathematical Sciences
- Teacher Education
- Pre-Professional Studies

Engineering Programs:

- Bachelor of Science in Manufacturing Engineering Technology
- Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering
- Bachelor of Science in Engineering (BSE)
- Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering
- Bachelor of Science in Manufacturing Engineering
- Bachelor of Science in Mechanical Engineering

Accreditation: The Bachelor of Science in Manufacturing Engineering (BSMFE) program is accredited by the Engineering Accreditation Commission (EAC) of ABET.

Manufacturing Engineering at USI:

- Focuses on applying engineering principles in the design of products and processes.
- Equips students for careers in various manufacturing enterprises.
- Requires advanced math skills in calculus and differential equations.
- Emphasizes oral and written communication skills.
- Offers hands-on experience and real-world competitions.

Opportunities and Competitions:

- Student chapters of professional engineering societies.
- Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineering (IEEE) robotics competition.
- SAE Baja competition for designing all-terrain vehicles.
- Solar Splash competition for building solar-powered electric boats.

Faculty and Classroom Experience:

- Taught by experienced engineering professors.
- Small class sizes and open-door policy for personalized interactions.
- Learn from faculty members who have practical industry experience.
