Post MSN APRN Certificate Program in the College of Nursing and Health Professions at the University of Southern Indiana (USI)

Overview:

    Program offered for Master's prepared students.
    Specialties: Adult-Gerontology Nurse Practitioner - Acute NP, Adult-Gerontology Clinical Nurse Specialist (AGCNS), Family Nurse Practitioner (FNP), Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner (PMHNP).
    Transcript evaluation and clinical core courses required for admission.
    Transfer credits of up to 6 credit hours considered.
    Study requirements: 3 to 5 hours per week per credit hour.
    Clinical courses have additional clinical hours.
    No campus visits required.
    Gainful employment information available.
    English proficiency requirements for non-native speakers.
    Accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education.

Admission Information:

    Program admission offered once a year.
    Fall and spring courses are 16 weeks long, while summer courses are 10 weeks long.
    No campus visits required.
    Additional information available on the About USI website.
    On-campus visits can be arranged through the USI Office of Admission.

Tuition Information, Handbooks, and Contact:

    Nursing & Health Professions Handbook and Student Handbook available.
    Tuition information provided.
    Contact information: Stone Family Science Center, 3037; Phone: 812-492-7807; Email provided on the site.

For more detailed information on each program's requirements and curriculum, please refer to the University's official website.
