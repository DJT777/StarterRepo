Pott College of Science, Engineering, and Education - Teaching Approach

Dr. Kent Scheller:

- Focuses on the student experience.
- Intention is to make the physics content intuitive and relatable to everyday experiences.
- Believes that understanding is better achieved through intuition rather than memorization.

Dr. Kenny Purcell:

- Sees himself as a guide on a journey through the course material and overall growth in understanding the Universe.
- Draws from personal experience and knowledge to navigate through the course.
- Uses various instructional methods, including lectures, demonstrations, problem-solving, and laboratory exploration.

Dr. Eric Greenwood:

- Utilizes a mixture of demonstration-based lectures, peer learning sessions, and multimedia presentations.
- Recognizes the importance of different learners having different expectations and needs.
- Finds dynamic ways to reach different learners while enhancing understanding and experience.

Dr. Jenna Kloosterman:

- Believes in the constructivist teaching principle, connecting new physics ideas to prior knowledge and real-world problem-solving.
- Encourages student participation, discussion, and sharing questions and thoughts in class.
- Designs assignments to help students see connections across physics concepts.

Dr. Matt Merlo:

- Aims for students to leave the class with a deeper understanding and appreciation for the subject.
- Tailors the presentation of material to each individual class, including interactive problem-solving and live demonstrations.
- Available outside the classroom to assist students individually and address their questions and concerns.
