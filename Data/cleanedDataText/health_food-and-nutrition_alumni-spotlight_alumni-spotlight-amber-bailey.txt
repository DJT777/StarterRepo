College of Nursing and Health Professions - Alumni Spotlight: Amber Bailey

Amber Bailey, MS, RDN, LDN, CNSC, is a Nutrition Support Specialist at a level 1 trauma center in Baton Rouge, Louisiana. She earned her Bachelor of Science in Food and Nutrition - Dietetics from the University of Southern Indiana (USI) in 2016.

Amber transferred to USI in her sophomore year after switching majors to dietetics. She was attracted to the smaller class sizes and the opportunity for more interactions with professors and fellow students.

The College of Nursing and Health Professions at USI prepared Amber for her career by challenging her and teaching her the importance of critical thinking, which has been instrumental in her current position. The college also served as a preceptor during her dietetic internship elective rotation, providing her with valuable experience in the academic sector of dietetics.

During her time at USI, Amber was involved in the Food and Nutrition Student Organization (FNSO) and Southern Roots Student Growers.

One of her favorite memories from her time at USI was her graduation, where she felt a sense of accomplishment and fulfillment in realizing her dream of becoming a registered dietitian.

Amber recommends USI to prospective students, highlighting the wonderful programs that prepare students for the professional world. She specifically mentions the improvements and cutting-edge curriculum in the dietetics program. She also emphasizes the benefits of smaller class sizes, which allow for meaningful interactions with diverse individuals and the opportunity to forge lifelong friendships.
