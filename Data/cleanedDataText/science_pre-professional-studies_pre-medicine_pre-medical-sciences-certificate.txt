Pre-Professional Pre-Medical Sciences Certificate

Overview:

- The Pre-Medical Sciences Certificate offered by the Pott College of Science, Engineering, and Education at the University of Southern Indiana (USI) provides excellent preparation for students seeking any medical-based pre-professional degree.
- It is available to students of any major and non-degree seeking students.
- The program offers pre-professional advising, guidance, and preparation for pre-professional exams.

Coursework:

- Students pursuing the Pre-Medical Sciences Certificate will complete a range of coursework that prepares them for pre-professional examinations and application to professional school.
- Courses include Principles of Biology, General Chemistry I & II, Organic Chemistry I & II, Cell Biology, Macromolecules & Metabolism or Biochemistry I, Physics I & II (with labs), Introduction to Psychology, Introduction to Sociology, and 5-8 hours of Biology or Chemistry electives.
- Electives must be unique from a student's BS or BA degree program and are selected in consultation with a Certificate advisor.
- Students must maintain a GPA of 3.00 or higher in all certificate coursework.

Student Support & Advising:

- Certificate students are eligible for financial aid and have access to a wide range of student support offered by USI.
- Students have access to mock MCAT and other pre-professional exams, HON 291 (a course aimed to help students prepare for their intended careers and applications), and advisors specializing in each pre-professional discipline.

How to Declare the Certificate:

- Degree-Seeking students: Complete the Change of Academic Program form available in the Biology Office or from the Registrar. Enter "Pre-Medical Sciences Certificate" in the "Add Major" section and submit the form to the Biology Office.
- Non-Degree Seeking students: Complete the Change of Status form available from the Registrar. This requires submitting high school and college transcripts. Submit the form to the Biology Office.
