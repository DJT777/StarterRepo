Types of Financial Aid at the University of Southern Indiana 

Student Financial Assistance

- Types of Aid: Grants, Loans, Work Study, Scholarships, Graduate Assistantships, 21st Century Scholars
- Applying for Aid: Information on how to apply for financial aid
- Managing Your Aid: Tips on how to manage your financial aid
- Cost of Attendance: Resources for understanding the cost of attending USI
- Frequently Asked Questions: Answers to common questions about financial aid

Grants

- State and federal grants available based on financial need and other situations
- Some grants have an application deadline of April 15

Loans

- Three major categories of loans: Federal Direct Loans, Federal Direct PLUS Loans, Private Education Loans
- Loans must be repaid

Scholarships

- Merit-based and need-based forms of aid that do not need to be repaid
- Scholarships available from the State of Indiana, USI, and other sources

Work Study

- Program providing jobs for undergraduate and graduate students in need of financial aid
- Students are paid at least minimum wage and work a designated number of hours per week

Assistantships (Graduate Students Only)

- Fee waivers for graduate students in exchange for work on campus
- Hourly wage is paid for hours worked

21st Century Scholars

- Program guaranteeing four years of college tuition at participating public colleges or universities in Indiana for income-eligible 7th- and 8th-graders who fulfill a pledge of good citizenship
