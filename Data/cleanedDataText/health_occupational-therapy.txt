Occupational Therapy Program at the College of Nursing and Health Professions (USI)

Degree and Program:

    Master of Science in Occupational Therapy (MSOT)
        Designed for students with a baccalaureate degree
        Entry-level graduate program in occupational therapy
        No preference for the major, as long as prerequisite courses are completed

Vision: Advancing health through meaningful occupations

Mission: To innovate through education, scholarship, and occupation-centered practice

Tuition and Fees: More information available on the USI website's financial aid section

NBCOT Program Results:
    Graduates of the MSOT Program must pass the National Board for Certification of Occupational Therapy (NBCOT) exam for state licensure and OT practice.
    Program results can be found on the NBCOT website.

Accreditation:
    The Occupational Therapy Program at USI is accredited by the Accreditation Council for Occupational Therapy Education (ACOTE) of the American Occupational Therapy Association (AOTA).
    Contact information for ACOTE provided.
