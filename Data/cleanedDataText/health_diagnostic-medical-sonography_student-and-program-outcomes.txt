        Program Outcomes:

            Minimum outcomes required by JRC-DMS for CAAHEP accredited sonography programs:

                Attrition
                Job Placement
                Credential Success Rates for Sonography Physics and Instrumentation (SPI), Abdomen (AB), Obstetrics and Gynecology (OB/GYN), Vascular Technology (VT), and Adult Echocardiography (AE)

            USI DMS Program Number: 110204

            Graduating Class:
                2014:
                    Attrition: 0%
                    Job Placement: 100%
                    Specialty Credentialing Exams: SPI (10/10), AB (10/10), OB/GYN (10/10), VT (5/5), AE (4/5)
                (Similar information provided for each year from 2015 to 2022)

            Programmatic Outcomes:

                Psychomotor Skills:
                    Understanding ultrasound production and interactions with patients
                    Operating imaging equipment properly and efficiently
                    Positioning patients and equipment for high-quality sonograms
                    Knowledge of medical image processing
                    Evaluating medical images for technical quality
                    Providing care and comfort to patients
                    Knowledge of human structure, function, and pathology
                    Assisting medical professionals in imaging performance
                    Knowledge of quality assurance and equipment evaluation

                Cognitive Skills:
                    Ensuring a safe environment for patients
                    Reacting to medical emergencies and difficulties
                    Providing patient education
                    Operating equipment within safe limits
                    Maintaining high quality in medical images

                Affective Skills:
                    Conduct consistent with professional and ethical standards
                    Compliance with the profession's scope of practice
                    Interacting cooperatively with health professionals
                    Providing care to patients with respect and without discrimination
                    Effective communication with patients and health professionals
