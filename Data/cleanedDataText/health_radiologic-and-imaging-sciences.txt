Radiologic and Imaging Sciences Program at the University of Southern Indiana (USI)

Program Overview:

USI offers a bachelor's degree in radiologic technology for two groups:

1. New undergraduate students
2. Currently credentialed imaging technologists

Job Market Advantage:

Since 2008, every graduate of USI's radiology program has found a job in radiologic technology within a year of graduation. The program provides more hands-on training and direct clinical experience compared to other radiology degree programs.

Highlights of the Program:

- Practical skills development in radiology
- Focus on building a professional demeanor and strong resume
- Direct clinical experience in radiology
- Small class sizes for extensive face-to-face instruction
- Training with state-of-the-art imaging equipment
- Learning advanced imaging software, including PACS
- Training in multiple radiologic technology specialties, such as computed tomography, magnetic resonance imaging, and special imaging procedures

Program Curriculum and Outcomes:

- Six practicums (or clinical courses) providing extensive hands-on experience in hospitals, outpatient clinics, and other healthcare facilities
- Comprehensive knowledge and skills in areas such as anatomy and physiology, positioning and procedures, radiation safety, digital image archiving and storage, and image analysis and critique
- Learn from faculty with years of experience in radiology and imaging technology

Accreditation:

- The Radiologic Technology Program at USI is fully accredited by the Joint Review Committee on Education in Radiologic Technology (JRCERT) for a period of eight years.
- The program meets the standards set by JRCERT for an accredited educational program in radiography.

Licensure and Certification:

- Upon completing the program and meeting the requirements of the American Registry of Radiologic Technologists (ARRT), graduates are eligible to apply for the national credentialing examinations administered by the ARRT.
- Students planning to pursue licensure or certification in a state other than Indiana should review the requirements and contact the state licensing board.
