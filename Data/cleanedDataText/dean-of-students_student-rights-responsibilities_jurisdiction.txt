Section 2: Jurisdiction
    - Students at the University/College are provided with a copy of the Student Rights and Responsibilities annually.
    - The Student Rights and Responsibilities apply to individual students, undergraduate and graduate, and all USI-affiliated student organizations.
    - USI considers an individual to be a student from the offer of admission until a continuing educational interest in USI exists.
    - Conduct jurisdiction is retained over students who take a leave of absence, withdraw, or graduate for any misconduct prior to these actions.
    - Sanctions may include holds on re-enrollment, official transcripts, and graduation until sanctions are satisfied.
    - Serious misconduct reported after graduation may result in the revocation of the student's degree.
    - The Student Rights and Responsibilities apply to on-campus behavior, USI-sponsored events, and off-campus conduct that affects a substantial university interest.
    - A substantial university interest includes situations that endanger health and safety, infringe on rights and property, breach the peace, cause social disorder, or harm USI's educational mission and interests.
    - The Student Rights and Responsibilities may be applied to online behavior.

Additional Information:
- The Student Rights and Responsibilities may also apply to guests of community members and non-students through contractual agreements.
- Visitors and guests of USI can seek resolution for violations committed against them by members of the university community.
- There is no time limit for reporting violations, but prompt reporting is encouraged for better investigation and response.
- Anonymous complaints are permitted but may limit the ability to investigate and respond to complaints.
- USI email is the primary means of communication with students, and they are responsible for checking their email regularly.
