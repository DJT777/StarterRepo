College of Nursing and Health Professions - Dental Assisting Program

Degree and Program Offerings:

- Dental Assisting
    - Certificate
    - Associate of Science degree

Program Details:

- Certificate Program: Requires eight credit hours of prerequisite coursework followed by one academic year (two semesters) of the program. Upon completion, students receive a Certificate in Dental Assisting, qualifying them to sit for the Dental Assisting National Board Certification Examination.
- Associate of Science Degree: A second year of study allows students to earn an associate of science degree with a major in dental assisting.

Additional Information:

- Core 39: In addition to departmental requirements, undergraduate students must complete the University Core 39, which provides a foundation for all baccalaureate degrees at the University of Southern Indiana. The core emphasizes discipline-specific competence and broader knowledge and skills across disciplines.
