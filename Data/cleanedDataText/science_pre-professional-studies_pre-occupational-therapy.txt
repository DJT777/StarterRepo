University of Southern Indiana (USI) - Pre-Occupational Therapy

Pre-Occupational Therapy Program:

- Overview:
  - USI offers a pre-occupational therapy program that allows students to start their OT track as freshmen.
  - Students can begin taking pre-occupational therapy courses immediately, a year earlier than in most OT programs.
  - Complete OT prerequisites and earn a bachelor's degree in exercise science within four years and 120 credit hours.
  - Specialized preparation that stands out on OT graduate school applications.

Opportunities in the Pre-OT Program:

- Professional Internship Experience:
  - Gain practical experience in occupational therapy clinics, hospitals, and rehab centers.

- Cutting-Edge Science and Equipment:
  - Train in the $300,000 Human Performance Laboratory with state-of-the-art equipment.

- OT Research Projects:
  - Participate in research projects related to occupational therapy.

- Experienced Faculty:
  - Take pre-OT courses taught by experienced faculty members, not graduate students.

- Pre-Professional Club:
  - Join the pre-professional club to connect with peers and professionals in the field.

Competitive Preparation:

- Admission to Graduate Schools:
  - Graduates of USI's pre-occupational therapy program have gained admission to top occupational therapy graduate schools, including USI's own master's degree program.

- Bachelor's Degree in Exercise Science:
  - Graduates earn a bachelor's degree in exercise science, providing strong preparation for OT graduate school.
