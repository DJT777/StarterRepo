Engineering Program Educational Objectives:

1. Program graduates will contribute to the engineering profession. This objective is considered achieved if, within five years, most graduates are working in engineering or in fields directly related to engineering.

2. Program graduates will contribute to the regional economy. This objective is considered achieved if, after five years, most graduates are employed within the tri-state region surrounding Evansville, Indiana.

Student Outcomes for the Bachelor of Science in Engineering align with ABET General Criterion 3 numbering 1 through 7, including:

1. Ability to identify, formulate, and solve complex engineering problems.
2. Ability to apply engineering design to produce solutions that meet specified needs.
3. Ability to communicate effectively.
4. Recognition of ethical and professional responsibilities in engineering.
5. Ability to function effectively in a team.
6. Ability to develop and conduct appropriate experimentation.
7. Ability to acquire and apply new knowledge.
