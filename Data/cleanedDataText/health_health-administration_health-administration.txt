Bachelor of Science in Health Administration (BSHA)

- The BSHA program at the University of Southern Indiana (USI) provides a comprehensive curriculum in healthcare administration for both acute and post-acute care settings.
- The program aims to prepare highly qualified entry-level graduates for career opportunities in healthcare administration.

Mission, Vision, and Values:

- Mission: The BSHA program delivers innovative education that prepares graduates for healthcare administration positions in acute, post-acute, and community care settings.
- Vision: To be a recognized leader in preparing future healthcare administrators who positively transform the health of the community.
- Values: Leadership, Critical Thinking, Forward Thinking & Innovation, Global Engagement, Compassion.

Degree Options:

- Bachelor of Science in Health Administration (major)
- Minor in Health Administration

Career Opportunities:

- Health Administrators manage day-to-day operations in healthcare organizations, leading interdisciplinary teams and improving organizational efficiency and healthcare delivery.
- Work settings: hospitals, physician practices, non-profits, post-acute care settings, government facilities.
- Job growth outlook: Jobs in health administration expected to grow by 32% by 2029 according to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics.

Faculty and Student Spotlights:

- Lauren Greiwe, a junior majoring in Health Administration, is the first student-athlete in USI school history to claim an NCAA Division I conference title.
