University of Southern Indiana (USI) Student Rights and Responsibilities

Authority:

The Dean of Students has the authority over student conduct, as granted by the Board of Trustees and President.
Works closely with the Assistant Director of Student Conduct and Outreach.
May appoint administrative hearing and appeals officers as necessary.

Gatekeeping:

No complaint will proceed to a hearing without reasonable cause to believe a policy has been violated.
Reasonable cause is defined as credible information supporting each element of the offense.
Complaints without any credible information will not proceed through the student conduct process.

Interpretation and Revision:

The Dean of Students establishes procedural rules for hearings consistent with the Student Rights and Responsibilities.
Material deviations require reasonable advance notice and may be made for legal or regulatory changes.
Minor modifications to procedure may be made that do not compromise fairness to any party.
The Dean of Students has final authority in interpreting the Student Rights and Responsibilities.
The Code of Student Conduct is updated annually and revised comprehensively every five years.

Approval and Implementation:

The Student Rights and Responsibilities were approved on August 14, 2023, by Dean Laurie Berry.
Implemented on August 15, 2023.

For more information, visit the USI website or contact the Dean of Students Office.
