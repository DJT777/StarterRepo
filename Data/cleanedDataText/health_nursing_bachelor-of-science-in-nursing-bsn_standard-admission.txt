College of Nursing and Health Professions - Undergraduate Admission Information

Application Process

Standard Admission:

1. Acceptance to the University of Southern Indiana as an undergraduate.
2. Application to the Bachelor of Science in Nursing Program at the College of Nursing and Health Professions.
3. Submission of a Personal Statement (150 words or less) explaining why you want to become a nurse.
4. Submission of the Leadership and Community Engagement Resume Form.
5. Participation in a personal interview.

Conditional Direct Admission:

1. Meet eligibility requirements for Direct Admission.
2. Completion of prerequisite second-year courses according to the curriculum.

Requirements

Academic Performance: 50.5 points

- GPA in required first-year prerequisite courses (ENG 101, ENG 201, BIOL 121, BIOL 122, CHEM 141, MATH 114, PSY 201, HP 115, UNIV 101, CMST 101 or CMS 107, KIN 192) with maximum possible 40 points.
- Additional points for grades in BIOL 121, BIOL 122, and CHEM 141.
- Anatomy and physiology and chemistry course credit must be earned within five years of enrollment in clinical nursing courses.

Personal Statement: 20 points

- Submission of a personal statement explaining why they want to become a nurse.

Interview: 30 points

- Participation in a personal interview.

Start of Nursing Coursework

- Students begin their nursing coursework in the spring semester of their second year of university study.

Maintenance of Eligibility

- Direct admission students must maintain a required cumulative GPA of 3.0 in first-year required courses.
- Achieve a grade of "C" or better in specific prerequisite courses.
- Maintain eligibility in science courses and nutrition courses.

Science Eligibility Rule

- Anatomy and physiology and chemistry course credit must be earned within five years of enrollment in clinical nursing courses.
- Students with three or more failures (a "D" or "F") in any of the required science courses are ineligible for the Nursing program at USI.
