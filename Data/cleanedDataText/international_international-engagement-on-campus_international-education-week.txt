International Education Week

- International Education Week is a national celebration held in November.
- Joint initiative of the U.S. Department of State and U.S. Department of Education.
- Purpose: promote programs that prepare Americans for a global environment and attract future leaders from abroad to study in the United States.
- Center for International Programs at USI participates in International Education Week.
- Aims to showcase the diverse campus and provide opportunities for students and faculty/staff to learn about the world.
- Students can pick up an IEW Passport and collect stamps for attending events.
- USI students attending at least 3 events and obtaining passport stamps are eligible to enter a drawing for prizes at the annual International Extravaganza.
- For more information, contact Emi Zlatkovska at ezlatkovsk@usi.edu.
