Cyber Security Certificate at USI Romain College of Business

Job Market Outlook:

- Forecasts show a high demand for Information Security Analysts in the job market.
- Cybersecurity roles take 21% longer to fill than other IT jobs.
- Employment in cybersecurity is projected to grow by 31% between 2021 and 2031.
- The median annual salary for cybersecurity analysts was $102,600 in 2021.

Scope and Specializations in Cybersecurity:

- The field of cybersecurity offers opportunities for specialization in various areas.
- Potential job titles include Cybersecurity Analyst, Cyber Crime Analyst, IT Auditor, Vulnerability Assessor, Cyber Forensics Investigator, Incident and Intrusion Analyst, Penetration Tester, and Cybersecurity Architect.

Coursework at USI:

- The cybersecurity curriculum at USI is constantly expanding to meet industry needs and national initiatives.
- Courses include Data Communications, Information Security and Risk Management, Cyber Security Assessment and Network Defense, and electives in topics such as Computer Forensics, Ethical Hacking, Cryptography, and more.

Cyber Security Operations Center (SOC):

- The Romain College of Business has a fully functional Cyber Security Operations Center (SOC).
- Students pursuing the certificate can work as analysts in the SOC, gaining real-world experience and helping to protect the USI campus.
