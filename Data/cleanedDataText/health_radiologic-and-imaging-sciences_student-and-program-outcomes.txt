College of Nursing and Health Professions

Radiologic and Imaging Sciences Program

Program Mission: The Radiologic Technology Program at the University of Southern Indiana aims to graduate students with advanced knowledge and skills in medical imaging. The program seeks to prepare students to be interdisciplinary team members for promoting the health and welfare of diverse patient populations.

Program Goals and Outcomes:

Goal One: Students will promote the health and welfare of patients through critical thinking.
- Outcome 1: Students will apply critical thinking skills to assess and adapt to different clinical presentation of patients.
- Outcome 2: Students will evaluate radiographic images for pathology relevant to medical imaging practice.

Goal Two: Students will demonstrate clinical competency as interdisciplinary team members.
- Outcome 1: Students will produce high-quality radiographic images using advanced technology and proper positioning techniques, while providing appropriate patient care.
- Outcome 2: Students will apply their knowledge of advanced patient assessment.

Goal Three: Students will demonstrate effective interpersonal skills.
- Outcome 1: Students will show effective communication skills with interdisciplinary teams to deliver care to diverse patient populations.
- Outcome 2: Students will apply effective interpersonal skills in a healthcare environment.

Job Placement Rate: The program reports the job placement rate of graduates who actively seek employment within twelve months of graduation. Non-active seekers are defined by the JRCERT as graduates who fail to communicate with program officials, are unwilling to relocate or accept employment due to salary or hours, are on active military duty, or continue education.
