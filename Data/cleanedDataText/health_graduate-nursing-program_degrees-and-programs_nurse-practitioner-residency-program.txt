University of Southern Indiana (USI) College of Nursing and Health Professions

Nurse Practitioner Residency Program

    Program Description:
        The College of Nursing and Health Professions at USI received a grant from the Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA) to develop a residency program for nurse practitioner graduates.
        The program focuses on family nurse practitioners (FNP) and psychiatric mental health nurse practitioners (PMHNP) who will serve rural and underserved populations in southwestern Indiana.
        Goals of the program include interprofessional education, utilization of telehealth for increased patient access, and continuous improvement through rapid cycle quality improvement.

    Application Information:
        Currently closed, applications are not being accepted at this time.

    Grant Information:
        The program is supported by a grant from HRSA (Health Resources and Services Administration) under the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services.
        The grant provides funding of $347,661 for year three of the program.
