Adult Geriatric Nurse Practitioner, Acute

    Program Information:

        Description: The Adult Geriatric Nurse Practitioner, Acute program focuses on providing direct health care services to late adolescents and adults across the lifespan. It emphasizes health promotion, disease prevention, and the management of acute and chronic illnesses.
        Clinical Settings: Graduates can work in various clinical settings such as acute care hospitals, long-term care facilities, physician offices, urgent care centers, and ambulatory care centers.
        Population: The program focuses on late adolescents through the elderly, addressing acute and episodic chronic health problems.
        Program Details:
            Total credit hours: 78
            Clinical hours: 1000

    Program Objectives:

        Synthesize knowledge from nursing theories, humanities, and evidence-based clinical guidelines to assess the health status of adults across the lifespan.
        Demonstrate advanced practice clinical decision making by integrating critical thinking skills to interpret patient and diagnostic test data, formulate differential diagnoses, and create a plan of care for adults across the lifespan.
        Design and implement a collaborative management plan and therapeutic interventions with adult patients and their families.
        Evaluate and revise management plans based on patient and family findings, problems, and treatment outcomes.
        Apply adult assessment methodologies and research findings to improve the care of adult patients and their families.
        Advocate for patients and families to provide cost-effective, culturally competent, ethical, and quality care across different healthcare settings.
        Model responsible professional development, integrity, accountability, competence, and credentialing as an acute care nurse practitioner.

    Curriculum Overview (78 Credit Hours):

        Graduate Nursing Courses: Includes courses on advanced nursing roles, evidence-based practice, theoretical foundations, analytical methods, cultural diversity, systems leadership, healthcare policy, resource utilization, informatics, wellness promotion, and more.
        DNP Project Courses: Covers proposal development and implementation of the final DNP project.
        Graduate Nursing Clinical Core Courses: Provides advanced concepts of pathophysiology, advanced health assessment, and clinical pharmacology.
        Specialty Courses: Focuses on the management of acutely ill adults through three levels.
