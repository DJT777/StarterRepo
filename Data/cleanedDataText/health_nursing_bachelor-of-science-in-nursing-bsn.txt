Application Process:

Direct Admission:
- For first-time degree-seeking students within the first calendar year of high school graduation.
- Requirements: High school junior year cumulative GPA of 3.5 or above on a 4.0 scale and minimum ACT of 26 or SAT of 1240.
- Maintain direct admission status by earning a college GPA of 3.0+ and achieving a grade of "C" or better in required first-year courses.

Conditional Direct Admission:
- For first-time degree-seeking students within the first calendar year of high school graduation.
- Requirements: High school junior year cumulative GPA of 3.4-3.49 on a 4.0 scale and one standardized exam score (SAT or ACT).
- Maintain eligibility by earning a college GPA of 3.3+ and completing first-year prerequisite courses.

Standard Admission:
- Admission after completion of 31 hours of required university courses.
- Admission requirements: Calculated GPA score, personal statement, leadership and community engagement resume, and personal interview.

Additional Information:
- Clinical Experiences: Nursing students will complete clinical experiences in various healthcare and community settings. Travel and transportation to assigned agencies may be required.
- English Proficiency: Non-native English speakers must meet English proficiency requirements.
- Mandatory Requirements: Drug screening, criminal background checks, TB, OSHA, and HIPAA training for students, faculty, and staff.
- Transfer Students: Transfer students must meet admission criteria and have their nursing courses evaluated for equivalency.
- Guidelines and Standards: The nursing program follows national guidelines and standards. Accredited by the Indiana State Board of Nursing and the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education (CCNE).
