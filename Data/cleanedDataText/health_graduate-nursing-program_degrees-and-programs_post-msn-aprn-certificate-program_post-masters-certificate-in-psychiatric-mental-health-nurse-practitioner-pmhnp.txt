Post Master's Certificate in Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner (PMHNP)

College of Nursing and Health Professions

Overview:
The Post Master's Certificate in Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner (PMHNP) is a program for advanced clinicians who have already obtained a Master of Science in Nursing. Graduates of this program provide psychiatric/mental health services to individuals of all ages. The role includes diagnosing, providing therapies, and managing medications for individuals with psychiatric/mental health problems. The PMHNP practices in various clinical settings such as hospitals, community agencies, long-term care facilities, home care, and outpatient offices.

Program Goals:
- Provide expert outcome-oriented nursing therapeutics for patients with mental health problems to promote improved health and enhanced quality of life.
- Manage, integrate, and coordinate patient care services across the mental health care continuum.
- Develop and provide patient and staff educational services to promote positive patient health care outcomes.
- Consult and collaborate with members of the multidisciplinary health care team to support the delivery of quality patient care.
- Incorporate best practice evidence to investigate, promote, and evaluate nursing therapies and pharmacotherapy that support evidence for positive patient care outcomes.
- Advocate for patients and families to provide cost-effective, culturally competent, ethical, quality care in a variety of health care settings.
- Model responsibility for continued professional development, integrity, accountability, competence, and credentialing as a family psychiatric mental health nurse practitioner.

Sample Plan of Study:
The program consists of both clinical core courses and specialty courses. The clinical core courses include Advanced Concepts of Pathophysiology, Advanced Health Assessment, and Clinical Pharmacology for Advanced Nursing Practice. The specialty courses focus on Psychopharmacology and Psychiatric/Mental Health Care of Families (I, II, and III). The total number of clinical hours required is 655.

Admission Requirements:
- Must hold a Master of Science in Nursing degree.
- Non-native English speakers must meet English proficiency requirements.
- The program is limited to certain states in the U.S. Check availability for out-of-state students.
- The baccalaureate degree in nursing, master's degree in nursing, Doctor of Nursing Practice, and post-graduate APRN certificate at USI are accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education.

Additional Information:
- The program accepts admissions once a year.
- Fall and spring courses are 16 weeks long, while summer courses are 10 weeks long.
- No campus visits are required.
- Tuition information and handbooks are available on the USI website.
