Food and Nutrition at USI

Mission and Goals:
- The mission of USI's Food and Nutrition program is to evaluate the role of nutrition in optimizing health throughout the lifespan and respond to harmful nutrition practices.
- Goals include demonstrating knowledge of nutrition as it applies to food service, dietetics, and wellness; applying management principles to nutrition and food service; presenting and evaluating food and nutrition education programs; assessing the role of behavior, economics, and dietary supplements in healthy eating; demonstrating basic food preparation and presentation skills; applying food science knowledge; and demonstrating problem-solving skills in food service, dietetics, and wellness.

Accreditation:
- The University of Southern Indiana is accredited by the Higher Learning Commission.

Programs Offered:
- Food and Nutrition - Dietetics Specialty Major
- Food and Nutrition - Food Service Management Specialty Major
- Food and Nutrition - Nutrition and Wellness Specialty Major
- Nutrition Minor

Careers:
- Graduates of the Food and Nutrition program at USI have a wide range of career options including dietitians, nutritionists, and food service managers.

Opportunities:
- The Food and Nutrition programs at USI are nationally recognized for their premier curriculums.
- The dietetics concentration is ACEND-accredited, and 100% of dietetics graduates who apply to a supervised practice program are admitted within 12 months of graduation.
- Hands-on, experiential learning opportunities are provided in state-of-the-art facilities like the Quantity Foods Kitchen and Clinical Simulation Center.
- Collaboration with students in other healthcare programs allows for real-world problem-solving and project work.
- Approximately 90% of graduates are employed in the state of Indiana.
- Employment of dietitians and nutritionists is projected to grow 7% from 2021 to 2031.
