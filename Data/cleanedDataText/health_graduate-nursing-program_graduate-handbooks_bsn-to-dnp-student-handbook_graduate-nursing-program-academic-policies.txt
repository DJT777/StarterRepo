College of Nursing and Health Professions

Academic Policies

Academic Integrity:
- Academic dishonesty (cheating, plagiarism, etc.) is not tolerated and may result in failing grades, dismissal from the nursing program, and the university.
- Policies and procedures can be found in the Student Rights and Responsibilities section of the University Bulletin.

Plagiarism:
- Intentionally using someone else's ideas, words, or statements without acknowledgment is prohibited.
- Proper crediting and referencing of sources is required.

Online Exams:
- Dates and times for online exams are clearly indicated in the course syllabus and calendar.
- Recommended internet browsers for online exams are Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, or Safari.
- Students must take practice exams and familiarize themselves with online platforms used in the program.
- Reliable internet connection and computer are the responsibility of the student.
- Remote exam proctoring may be required.

Proctored Exams:
- Some courses may require proctored exams in the MSN and Post MSN Certificate programs.
- Requirements for exam proctoring software can be found on the Online Learning website.

Student Assignments:
- Student papers/assignments may be used as examples for course files and accreditation purposes with permission.
- Sharing assignments, exams information, and graded work is considered academic dishonesty.

Technology Proficiency:
- Competency in using computers and software (Excel, Microsoft Word, Power Point) is required.
- Access to a reliable internet connection is necessary for distance education components.

Professional Behavior:
- Professional behavior is expected in online, classroom, and clinical environments.
- Professional Behavioral Agreement Statements must be signed and uploaded as part of the program requirements.

Preparation for Exams and Board Certification:
- Diagnostic exams and predictor exams may be required for clinical courses and board certification preparation.
- Self-remediation is expected for poor performance to improve clinical performance and certification pass rates.

Change of Name/Address/Phone:
- Changes must be reported immediately to the University Registrar's Office and the Nursing Programs.

Statistics Proficiency:
- Competence in reading, analyzing, and interpreting statistical data is expected.

Writing Proficiency:
- Written assignments must follow APA style format and demonstrate logical flow of ideas, proper grammar, spelling, and sentence structure.

Nursing & Health Professions Handbook:
- Provides additional information and guidelines for the program.
