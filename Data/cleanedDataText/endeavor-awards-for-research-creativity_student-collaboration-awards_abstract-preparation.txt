How to Write an Abstract:

    Purpose: An abstract is a brief summary of your project, typically fewer than 350 words. It is used to give readers a synopsis of your research or creativity project.

    Components of an Abstract:

        Project Statement: Describe the problem, task, or goal your project addresses and how it will benefit you, your field, and the world.

        Creative Projects: Explain why you chose to produce your specific piece of work and how it expands your field. Discuss the process and include an interpretation and feedback from the audience.

        Scientific, Business, or Academic Area Projects: State the problem your project addresses and the data, methodology, or techniques used. Provide an interpretation of the data.
