Pre-Physical Therapy FAQ - Pott College of Science, Engineering, and Education

What major should I choose?
    - No specific undergraduate major is required for admission to a DPT program.
    - Common majors among PT students include exercise science, biology, kinesiology, and psychology.

What classes do I need to take?
    - PT school requirements vary, but most require classes such as anatomy and physiology, chemistry, physics, statistics, and psychology.
    - For more information on individual program requirements, refer to the PTCAS website.

Do I need a 4.0 GPA?
    - No, the average GPA for matriculating students is 3.5, with a 3.0 in math and science courses.

How do I apply?
    - Visit the centralized application service, PTCAS, for application information.

Do I need observation hours?
    - Observation hours are usually required for pre-physical therapy training.
    - The number of hours required may vary between 0-200, depending on the program.
    - Check the PTCAS Directory for specific program requirements.

Tips to Remember:
    - Start observation hours early, even if not required, to gain insight into the physical therapy field.
    - Be aware of application deadlines for each program.
    - Some programs have rolling admissions, so applying early is beneficial.
    - Request transcripts from all academic institutions attended, including dual credit awards from high school.
    - Choose letter writers carefully and provide them with ample time to submit their letters.
    - When requesting letters of evaluation, be prepared to provide your CV and personal statement.
