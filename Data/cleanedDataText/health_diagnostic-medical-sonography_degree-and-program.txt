The Diagnostic Medical Sonography (DMS) Program at the University of Southern Indiana:
- Provides a curriculum consisting of didactic courses, laboratory and clinical experiences.
- Consists of 1 1/2 years of prerequisite general education courses, 1 1/2 years of abdominal and OBGYN sonography coursework, and one year of either vascular sonography or cardiac sonography coursework.
- Leads to a Bachelor of Science degree.
- Allows students to be eligible to apply for credentialing examinations administered by the American Registry for Diagnostic Medical Sonography (ARDMS).
- Prerequisite courses must be completed with a grade of C or better.
- More information and degree requirements can be found in the USI Bulletin.
