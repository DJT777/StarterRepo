Diagnostic Medical Sonography Program at the University of Southern Indiana (USI)

Overview:
The Diagnostic Medical Sonography program at USI offers specialized training in using sound waves to create diagnostic images for medical purposes. Sonographers work under the direction of physicians and perform sonographic examinations of various parts of the body. The program is accredited by the Commission on Accreditation of Allied Health Education Programs (CAAHEP) and the Joint Review Committee on Education in Diagnostic Medical Sonography (JRC-DMS).

Program Licensure/Certification:
- Upon completion of the program, graduates are eligible to apply for national credentialing examinations administered by the American Registry for Diagnostic Medical Sonography.

Faculty Recognition:
- Amy Wilson, Clinical Associate Professor and Chair of Diagnostic Medical Sonography, and Dr. Katherine Peak, Clinical Assistant Professor, received the 2023 Faculty Development Award for the College of Nursing and Health Professions.
- They used the award to purchase a 3D/4D transducer for the program's new ultrasound machine, providing advanced technology for obstetric and gynecologic imaging.
- The funding also included training for faculty and students.

Career Opportunities:

Pregnancy Ultrasounds:
- The USI Diagnostic Medical Sonography program offers non-diagnostic ultrasounds to expectant mothers in their second or third trimester (18-34 weeks pregnant).
- Students practice assigned images under the supervision of experienced faculty members.
- Appointments can be scheduled by emailing usi1dms@usi.edu.
