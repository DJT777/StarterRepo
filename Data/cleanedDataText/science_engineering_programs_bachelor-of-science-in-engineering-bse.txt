Bachelor of Science in Engineering (BSE) Program at the Pott College of Science, Engineering, and Education

Accreditation:
- The BSE program is accredited by the Engineering Accreditation Commission (EAC) of ABET.

Notable Achievement:
- In 2016, the College received funding to design, test, deploy, and monitor a satellite for NASA called UNITE CubeSat.
- The satellite was launched by NASA on a SpaceX rocket in December 2018 and deployed from the International Space Station in January 2019.
- UNITE CubeSat relays information on location, temperature, and plasma until its reentry into the atmosphere.

Program Overview:
- The BSE program is the flagship baccalaureate degree in engineering at the Pott College.
- Students concentrate their studies in one of five emphasis areas: mechatronics, mechanical, civil, industrial and manufacturing, and electrical and computer.
- Faculty members have experience in both industry and academia, providing a practical and theoretical education.
- Class sizes are small, allowing for student-teacher interaction.

Bioengineering and Career Opportunities:
- Indiana's orthopedic device industry presents opportunities for bioengineering talent.
- Graduates find employment in various industries.
- The degree prepares students for professional licensure as a engineer (PE).
- Students have the opportunity to work on the development of a CubeSat for NASA.

Engineering Affiliations and Experiential Learning:
- The College offers extracurricular opportunities for practical experience, such as the UNITE CubeSat project.
- The College has professional affiliations that provide additional resources and networking opportunities for students.
