Intensive English Program Placement and Assessment

Placement:
- CaMLA placement test (including speaking test and writing section) administered to determine the appropriate level of instruction.
- Instructors may make placement adjustment recommendations based on student performance during the first week of class.
- At the end of each session, students in levels 4 and 6 will take the itpTOEFL tests to measure readiness for more academic instruction at level 4 or completion of the IEP at level 6.
- Dissatisfied students can submit a Placement Modification Form to the IEP Director for further evaluation.
- A committee may conduct an unofficial Oral Proficiency Interview (OPI) process, considering basic information questions, word listings, visual-based questions, description/narration in past, present, and future, and response to a prompt on a current topic. Recommendations from teachers and instructors will also be considered.

Assessment:
- Formative assessment includes projects, quizzes, mid-term and final exams, participation, and homework.
- Failing one class within a level may require repeating the whole level.
- IEP teachers apply a level promotion policy to determine readiness for promotion to the next level.
- Students graduating from the Intensive English Program receive a certificate of completion.
- Exceptions will be considered on a case-by-case basis.
