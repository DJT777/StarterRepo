Deferral Policy for International Admissions at University of Southern Indiana (USI)

Deferral Option:
International students are eligible to defer their admission or application one time for up to one academic year.

Process for Application Deferral:
1. Online Application: For students who have completed the online application.
2. Priority Deferral: Submit deferral requests before the first day of class of the term you applied for.
3. Application Deferral: Can be granted once and up to one year from the original application start date.
4. Evaluation: Coursework completed since the application was submitted will be reviewed in the deferral process, and a new application may be required.

Process for Admission Deferral:
1. Application and Acceptance: For students who have applied and been accepted to USI.
2. Priority Deferral: Submit deferral requests before the first day of class of the term you were admitted.
3. Admission Deferral: Not automatic or guaranteed; all requests will be reevaluated for admission.
4. Maximum Deferral: A maximum of two consecutive semesters (excluding summers) may be granted.
5. Specific Programs: The admission deferral does not necessarily defer admission to a particular major or program of study.
6. Case-by-Case Basis: Requests to defer an offer of admission will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis and may be granted for reasons such as visa denial, illness, military service, and humanitarian or religious service. Supporting documentation must be provided.
