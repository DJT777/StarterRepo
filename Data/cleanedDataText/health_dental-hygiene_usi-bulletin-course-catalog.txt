Dental Hygiene Program

Overview:

- Dental hygiene involves the use of a process of care to assess oral health status, develop a dental hygiene diagnosis, and plan and implement preventive and therapeutic services to achieve optimum oral health.
- Career opportunities for licensed dental hygienists are available in dental clinics and practices, administrative and managerial healthcare, education, research, and public health.
- The dental hygiene program at USI provides a curriculum that includes didactic courses, laboratory and clinical experiences, and community oral health education experiences.

Admission Requirements:

- Acceptance to the University of Southern Indiana.
- Completion of required prerequisite college courses with a grade of C or higher and a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.8.
- Submission of a dental hygiene application.
- Submission of medical and immunization records.
- CPR certification for healthcare providers.
- Completion of a criminal background check.
- Completion of drug testing.

Application Process:

- Application materials are available in the College of Nursing and Health Professions office and on the program's website.
- The completed application must be submitted to the dental hygiene program by February 1 of the desired admission year.
- Applications received after February 1 will be considered on a space-available basis.
- Admission into the dental hygiene program is limited to 24 students each academic year. The selection is based on the cumulative GPA in the required prerequisite courses, with the top 24 students being offered admission.

Important Note:

- Prerequisite courses must be completed with a grade of C or better and cannot be taken on a pass/no pass option.
- Dental hygiene courses taken while enrolled in the University of Southern Indiana dental assisting program must be completed within three years of entering the dental hygiene program.
