College of Nursing and Health Professions - Dental Assisting Program

Prepare Yourself for a Dental Assisting Career:

- Dental assistants are integral members of the oral care health team, performing procedures at chairside, in the laboratory, and the dental business office.
- USI dental assisting graduates possess knowledge and understanding of legal, ethical, and professional standards of dental assisting, as well as clinical skills to function in general and specialty dental practices.

Career Outlook:

- Dental assisting is one of the fastest-growing professions, with a projected growth rate of 25% in the next 10 years.
- Demand for dental assistants is increasing due to population growth and an aging population in the United States.

Program Accreditation and Licensure:

- The Dental Assisting program is accredited by the Commission on Dental Accreditation of the American Dental Association.
- Certification is not required for employment in the state of Indiana, and the university has not determined if the program meets certification requirements in other states.

Opportunities and Benefits:

- USI's Dental Assisting program offers the unique opportunity to receive both a Certificate of Dental Assisting and an Associate of Science degree upon graduation.
- Dental assisting courses are taught by knowledgeable faculty with years of experience in the profession.
- Individualized instruction and clinical rotations provide valuable workplace experience and the opportunity to build relationships within the community.
- Development of critical thinking, communication, and professional skills.

Program Statistics and Achievements:

- 97% of graduates earn both their Associate of Science degree and their Certificate of Dental Assisting.
- 95% of graduates become Certified Dental Assistants.
- 100% of graduates are able to obtain employment in a dental office.
