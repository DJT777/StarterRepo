    Admission Information:

        Direct Admission to the Nursing Program:
            - Acceptance to the University of Southern Indiana as an undergraduate.
            - Non-native English speakers must meet English proficiency requirements.
            - Complete and submit the Early Admission Application.
            - Start nursing coursework in the spring semester of the second year of university study.
            - Eligibility maintenance requirements: Earn a college GPA of 3.3+ on a 4.0 scale, complete first-year prerequisite courses.
            - First Year Courses: ENG 101, ENG 201, BIOL 121, BIOL 122, CHEM 141 (or alternative options), MATH 114, PSY 201, HP 115, UNIV 101, CMST 101 or 107, KIN 192.

        Conditional Direct Admission to the Nursing Program:
            - Same requirements as Direct Admission.
            - Maintain a required cumulative GPA of 3.0 on first-year required courses.
            - Achieve a grade of "C" or better in specified courses.
            - Science eligibility: Anatomy and physiology and chemistry courses must be completed or equivalent competence demonstrated within five years of enrollment in clinical nursing courses.

        Standard Admission to the Nursing Program:
            - Traditional application process.
            - Specific prerequisites and admission requirements apply.
