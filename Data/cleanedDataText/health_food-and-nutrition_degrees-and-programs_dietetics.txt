Food and Nutrition Program

Degrees and Programs:

- Dietetics
- Food Service Management
- Nutrition and Wellness

Program Mission, Goals, and Objectives:

- Prepare students for supervised practice leading to eligibility for the Registered Dietitian Nutritionist (RDN) credential.
- Provide education and service learning opportunities for students to maximize their potential and meet the needs of the changing field of dietetics.
- Promote a collaborative research environment.
- Support community engagement through service, continuing education, and consultation activities.
- Support the professional career aspirations of students, faculty, and alumni.

Course of Study:

- Minimum of a bachelor's degree with coursework approved by the Accreditation Council for Education in Nutrition and Dietetics (ACEND).
- Completion of a supervised practice program.
- Pass the national registration exam to become a Registered Dietitian Nutritionist (RDN).

Admission Criteria:

- Complete specified prerequisite coursework with a minimum 2.9 overall GPA.
- Complete an online Dietetic Program admission application.

Requirements for Graduation:

- Complete University Core 39 requirements.
- Complete required nutrition core courses.
- Complete Dietetics option courses.
- Complete a minimum of 120 credit hours, with at least 30 credit hours taken at USI.
- Maintain a minimum grade point average of 2.0.
- Achieve a grade of C or better in core nutrition and dietetic courses.

Career Opportunities:

- Employment opportunities in healthcare, business, public health, education, research, and private practice.
- Registered Dietitians work in hospitals, HMOs, community health settings, schools, sports nutrition, and more.

Program Accreditation:

- The Dietetic Program in Dietetics at the University of Southern Indiana is accredited by the Accreditation Council for Education in Nutrition and Dietetics (ACEND).

Equal Opportunity Commitment:
