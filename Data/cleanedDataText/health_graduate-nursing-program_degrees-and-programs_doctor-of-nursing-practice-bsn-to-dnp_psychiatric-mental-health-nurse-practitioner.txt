Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner (PMHNP) Online Program

Information about PMHNP Program:

- The PMHNP program is designed for advanced clinicians who have earned a Master of Science in Nursing.
- PMHNPs provide psychiatric/mental health services to individuals across the lifespan, including diagnosis, therapy, and medication management.
- They practice in various settings such as acute care and chronic care hospitals, community agencies, long-term care facilities, home care, and outpatient offices.
- The program consists of 80 credit hours and 1000 clinical hours.
- Graduates are prepared to provide expert nursing therapeutics, coordinate patient care services, deliver patient and staff educational services, consult and collaborate with the multidisciplinary healthcare team, incorporate evidence-based practice, advocate for patients and families, and model professional development and integrity.

Program Curriculum - 80 Credit Hours:

- Graduate Nursing Courses: include roles for advanced nursing practice, evidence-based practice, theoretical foundations, analytical methods, cultural diversity, systems leadership, healthcare policy, resource utilization, informatics, wellness promotion, evidence-based practice seminar, patient management clinical application seminar, applications in population health, applications in pharmacotherapeutics, health policy application in APN, and quality initiatives in nursing.
- DNP Project Courses: include DNP proposal and DNP project.
- Graduate Nursing Clinical Core Courses: include advanced concepts of pathophysiology, advanced health assessment, and clinical pharmacology.
- Specialty Courses: include psychopharmacology, psychiatric/mental health care of families I and II, and family psychiatric mental health nurse practitioner practicum III.

Sample Plan of Study and Other Specialty Tracks:

- A sample plan of study is available for reference.
- Additionally, other specialty tracks offered include adult geriatric nurse practitioner, acute adult gerontologic clinical nurse specialist, family nurse practitioner, and organizational and systems leadership.
