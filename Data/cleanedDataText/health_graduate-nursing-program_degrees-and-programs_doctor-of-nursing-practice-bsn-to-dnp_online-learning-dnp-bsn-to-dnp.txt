USI Doctor of Nursing Practice (DNP) - BSN to DNP

Program Overview:
- Complete in 4-5 years
- 100% online
- Total of 78 credit hours
- Full- and part-time options available

Specialty Tracks:
- Adult Geriatric Nurse Practitioner, Acute
- Adult Gerontologic Clinical Nurse Specialist
- Family Nurse Practitioner
- Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner

Admission Requirements:
- Baccalaureate degree in nursing (BSN) from a nationally-accredited nursing program and regionally-accredited college or university
- Minimum cumulative baccalaureate GPA of 3.00 on a 4.00 scale
- Completion of a statistics course with a grade of C or better
- Unencumbered US RN license in all states of practice

Application Procedures:
- Submit online application and $40 application fee
- Upload current professional resume or curriculum vitae
- Submit transcripts to Graduate Studies (official transcripts required from highest degree-earning institution)
- International applicants may need to provide additional information

Career Opportunities:
- Scholarly practice: Conduct research, clinical trials, and publish findings
- Nursing education: Teach at the community college or university level
- Health care leadership: Serve as an advocate or expert in the community, state, or country
- Nursing care specialization: Pursue advanced practice roles in various settings

Program Outcomes:
- Integrate nursing science with knowledge from ethics and various sciences for the highest level of nursing practice
- Develop and evaluate care delivery approaches based on scientific findings
- Use analytic methods to appraise existing literature and implement the best evidence for practice
- Serve as a leader in the development and implementation of health care policies
- Advocate for patients and the nursing profession
- Collaborate with interprofessional teams to analyze complex practice and organizational issues
- Analyze scientific data related to individual, aggregate, and population health
- Utilize advanced judgment, systems thinking, and accountability in delivering evidence-based care

Flexible Online Learning:
- Interactive online management system to enhance learning
- Real-time recorded student engagement in labs
- Full-time completion in 4 years, part-time completion in 5 years
- Support from faculty advisor, faculty mentor, and clinical preceptor

Accreditation: 
- Doctor of Nursing Practice program accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education
